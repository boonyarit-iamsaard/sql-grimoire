# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The target users are working developers who are comfortable writing everyday SQL and seek genuine
database mastery across querying, schema design, constraints, transactions, concurrency, indexing,
query plans, migrations, and ORM behavior. They use the product independently in a browser during self-directed learning time. Their objective
is to solve a realistic production incident and finish with an understanding of _why_
the solution works. The current audience consists of playtesters who answer whether completing
mission one makes them want mission two.

## Product Purpose

SQL Grimoire is a browser-based SQL-learning RPG: an embedded database,
result-graded challenges, and a narrative world where missions are production
incidents framed as quests. Today it is a complete but unvalidated one-mission prototype; the
long-term goal is a commercially viable database-mastery platform for individual developers.
Team, bootcamp, and company sales are a possible later expansion, considered only if individual
traction proves out. Success for the prototype is a playtester completing "The Missing Shipment"
and expressing interest in another mission.

## Brand Personality

The brand resembles a welcoming arcane scholar in a warm, candlelit study, accompanied by a
grimoire, a patient mentor, and quiet magic. Learning should feel like a ritual that encourages
calm curiosity and earned confidence without pressure or noise. The three defining qualities are
warmth, scholarship, and subtle magic.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate learning management systems** use course-card grids, progress percentages, and
  certificates in the style of Coursera or Udemy.
- **Superficial gamification** uses streaks, confetti, and persistent mascot prompts in the style
  of Duolingo without integrating those elements into the learning experience.
- **Generic software-as-a-service dashboards** use sidebars, statistic cards, charts, and default
  dark administrative templates.
- **Grimdark fantasy** relies on skulls, blood-red colors, and oppressive darkness to create an
  excessively serious fantasy setting.

## Design Principles

1. **The error message is the mentor.** SQLite's output is the pedagogy — never
   hide, soften, or gate it behind client-side validation.
2. **Workbench first, story as candlelight.** Narrative frames the work and
   then gets out of the way; the editor, results, and schema are the primary
   surface on every mission screen.
3. **Earned, quiet rewards.** Completing a mission feels like mastering a
   spell. The reward should feel substantial and satisfying without conspicuous celebratory effects,
   and its significance should correspond to the required effort.
4. **Purpose-built assets.** All art is authored SVG, all sound is
   synthesized; every decorative element should feel drawn for this world.
5. **Calm density.** The interface should provide the precision of a developer tool through crisp
   monospace surfaces and readable result grids without dashboard ornamentation.

## Accessibility and Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: ≥4.5:1 body-text contrast, full keyboard operability across
the workbench, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation, and
no information conveyed by sound alone.
